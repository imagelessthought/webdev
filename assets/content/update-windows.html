Updating your Windows Device

This tutorial provides step-by-step instructions for installing updates on your Windows device.

It is important to keep your device up to date to ensure there are no security vulnerabilities present on your device. Devices that are on-campus, such as the computer lab and classroom computers, are updated each night. With laptops, the updates are not necessarily installed as reliably so we ask that you check the status regularly. If your device has not been updated in a while, we will contact you by email and ask you to follow these steps. In some cases, updates are not being received due to an issue with the device and we may need to have you contact the service desk to begin a remote troubleshooting session.

Be aware that once you begin the update process, any application that you have running will be closed in order to install updates. Make sure you save your work before beginning the update and do not work on your computer while the update is running.

Your computer will restart once the update has completed. It generally does not take more than half an hour to complete an update.

Using Software Center to Update you Device

On your Windows device, you can install the updates for the operating system, Microsoft Office 365, Adobe Creative Cloud and your web browsers all in one place by using Software Center. Software Center is installed on all Windows devices supported by the college.

Use the search bar at the bottom left of your screen, next to the Start Menu button and type in Software Center, or as much as is needed to bring up the Software Center App

The update is listed under Applications. Look for the application labelled, "Update This PC", and once you are ready to begin, press Install.

That is all there is to the update process. Once your machine reboots, log back in and you can begin working on your device again.

Thank you for helping us keep your device secure.
