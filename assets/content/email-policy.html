Employees: Access to UNT Resources

University employees receive access to various resources and equipment as part of their employment at UNT. These are paid for by UNT (or through a grant) and belong to the university, as do the materials and any information stored on them, once the individual leaves UNT. These resources and equipment are provided for your work as a UNT employee and are not for personal use.

Once an individual leaves UNT, they will no longer have access to these resources: this includes but is not limited to UNT email account(s) and the Adobe Creative Cloud subscription (and the files contained on the Adobe Cloud).

Any backups should be done prior to an employee's final day with the university. We also suggest departing employees put a permanent out-of-office reply on email accounts notifying people that they have left UNT and provide new contact information prior to their final day. Supervisors should ensure access to files and other resources needed by their department is not lost upon the employee's departure.

Adjuncts who do not teach courses in both long semesters (Fall and Spring) will only have access to these resources while they are actively employed by the college or university. In regard to their UNT Email: plan ahead by setting up an out-of-office reply if you are not returning for the next long semester.

Some things to be aware of from the Office of Information Security and Compliance:

  • The accounts are owned by UNT and files that are associated are also owned by UNT. It is a policy violation to use university accounts for personal use, and to store university owned information in personal accounts.
  • Individuals that are not actively employed by the university are not entitled to use or have access to any content in a university account, system or resource. This applies across the board.
  • Information stored on a university resource is considered the property of the University. Once the separation date has been reached the employee is not authorized to access the information.

Special consideration is given to retirees: this will be discussed during an employee's offboarding.
