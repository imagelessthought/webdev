The CVAD Computer Lab is availale to all students attending classes on the UNT Campus. The equipment, software and services provided are intended to specifically serve the needs of students studying in the arts and design programs. Equipment including 3D scanners and printers, large format printers, and photographinc scanners and printers are available in the lab and equipment such as cameras and Wacom design tablets are available for checkout by students.

The Digital Fabrication Lab is available to all students actively enrolled in courses in the College of Visual Arts and Design. We provide hands-on access to the latest equipment: 3D printers, CNC Routers, Laser Cutters and more. The equipment is available by appointment to students at no additional cost.

The College of Visual Arts and Design provides an online service desk, using Microsoft Teams, for our students and employees. We are available during normal business hours to answer any questions you may have and provide remote technical support, whether you are on or off-campus, without you having to physically come by or bring your computer to our office.

The College of Visual Arts and Design manages the Mac portion of the MyLab: UNT Online Computer Lab which provides remote access to Mac computers with the software required to complete the courses offered by our college. Microsoft Windows applications are availble as well, without the need to use a Windows Computer, through integration with Citrix (provided by the G. Brint Ryan College of Business).

Bridge provides most of the required institutional training for things such as the Annual Security Awarenesss, Inventory Management, ePro, Performance Management, and the UNT Ethics and Standards of Conduct. The courses in Bridge are designed to help you learn and keep you up to date on the various processes, procedures, and policies related to an employee's job duties.

LinkedIn Learning is provided free of charge to UNT System students, faculty and staff. You can browse through more than 10,000 courses. Courses are available in several languages. The video-based courses cover a wide-range of topics: business, technology and creative skills. Courses are viewable on your computer's web browser or via LinkedIn Learning's mobile apps for iOS and Android Devices. Fun fact: In 2015, when the service was stilled called Lynda.com, CVAD was the first unit in the UNT System to provide access to all of our students, faculty and staff.

We use Microsoft products pretty extensively these days, even our Mac users. Onedrive, Teams, Sharepoint, are all part of the Office 365 suite of applications. There are many more and they are used extensivley by faculty, staff, and students. Microsoft provides some useful tutorials that you may find useful: https://support.microsoft.com/en-us/training. Next year we will migrating our Windows users to the latest version of the Windows operating system: Windows 11. You will probably become very familar with this site at that time, if not sooner. You may also be interested in their Learning Academy: all of the online courses are free.

In the College of Visual Arts and Design, Apple is the dominant platform for instructional, research, and faculty computing. Apple devices are also highly preferred as personal devices as well, and this is true for faculty, staff, and students. Apple provides user guides for all of their devices, the latest operating systems (MacOSX and iOS), and their own proprietary software applications such as iWork and Final Cut Pro, that you may find useful: https://support.apple.com/guide/mac-help.
